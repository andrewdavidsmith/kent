Description

The Genome in a Bottle (GIAB) Problematic Regions tracks provide stratifications of the genome to evaluate variant calls in complex regions. It is designed for use with GA4GH benchmarking tools like hap.py and includes regions with low complexity, segmental duplications, functional regions, and difficult-to-sequence areas. Developed in collaboration with GA4GH and the Genome in a Bottle (GIAB) consortium, the dataset aims to standardize the analysis of genetic variation by offering pre-defined BED files for stratifying true and false positives in genomic studies, facilitating accurate assessments in complex areas of the genome.

Methods

The creation of the GIAB Problematic Regions tracks involves using a pipeline and configuration to generate stratification BED files that categorize genomic regions based on specific challenges, such as low complexity or difficult mapping, to facilitate accurate benchmarking of variant calls. For more information on the pipeline and configuration used, please visit the following webpage: https://ftp-trace.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/ReferenceSamples/giab/release/genome-stratifications/v3.5/README.md.
